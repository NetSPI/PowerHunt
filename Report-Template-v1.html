PowerHunt Process

The PowerHunt audit script was run against the provided targets. PowerHunt is an open-source modular threat hunting framework written in PowerShell that leverages PowerShell Remoting for data collection on scale. It was designed to identify signs of compromise based on artifacts left behind by common MITRE ATT&CK techniques, and the collected data can be used to identify anomalies and outliers specific to the target environment. PowerHunt generated this HTML summary report and the associated html/csv files to help enable analysts at the beginning of their threat hunting exercise.

How do I use this report?

Follow the guidance below to get the most out of this report.

Review the reports and data insights to get a quick feel for the volume of potentially malicous articfacts and anomalies in your environment.

Reports
The Target, Collection, and Analysis summary sections will provide a summary of the results.

Data Insights
The Data Insights sections are intented to highlight natural data groupings that can help centralize and expedite the identification of potentially malicous articfacts and anomalies in your environment.
Review potentially collected data and initial analysis stored in the associated HTML and CSV files.
Review the definitions below to ensure you understand what was targeted and how privileges have been qualified as excessive.

Suspicious Artifacts
"Suspicious Artifacts", are artifacts that are associated with behavior known to be malicious. Artifacts include, but are not limited to events, files, registry keys, wmi subscriptions, wmi providers, tasks, services, and correlations that rely on meta data from collection data sources.

Environment Anomalies
Environmental anomalies are statistical outliers within the environment that may or may not be associated with malicous behavior.
Follow the guidance in the Remediation sections for general guidance for review the data generated by PowerHunt.
Collect data and generate this HTML report by running the PowerHunt audit script.
The command examples below can be used to identify potentially malicious share permissions. Additional command examples can be found here.

From Domain System
Invoke-PowerHunt -OutputDirectory "c:\temp" -Threads 100

From Non-Domain System
Invoke-PowerHunt -OutputDirectory "c:\temp" -Threads 100 -DomainController 10.1.1.1 -Credential domain\user
